Overview

  • Korean martial art
  • Focuses on forms, board breaks, sparring, weapons, and self defense
  • Has 13+ belts before black belt
  • Allows people to progress up to a ninth degree balck belt
  • Demonstrate skills in some combination of forms, sparring, board breaks, weapons, and self-defense

Forms

  • A set of movements used to demonstrate technique
  • Consists of kicks, hand movements, and other fighting motions
  • Progresses in difficulty with each belt

Board Breaks

  1. Side kick
  2. Front kick
  3. Hammer fist
  4. Round kick

Self-Defense

  • Learning how to defend youself in the real world using martial arts skills
  • Work with a partner to practice getting out of dangerous situations
  • Learn how to defend yourself from people grabbing you, choking you, etc.

Weapons

  1. Swords
  2. Staffs
  3. Ssahng Jeol (Nunchucks)
  4. Bahng Mahng Ee (Single Stick)

Sparring

  • Combat with others
  • Gear is required
  • Demonstrate techniques learned in class

Demonstrations

  1. Graduation
    • Used for lower ranked belts
    • Demonstration of skills learned during a cycle
    • Used to show off skills for students who know they're moving on to the next belt
  2. Testing
    • Used for higher ranked belts
    • Demonstrate skills learned in front of judges
    • Judges decide during testing whether you progress to the next belt or not
  3. Competitions
    • Competing with other students
    • Can compete in many different categories (sparring, forms, weapons, etc.)
    • A winner is decided based on judges rankings
