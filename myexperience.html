My Experience

My journey through Taekwondo

I started Taekwondo in 2019, when I was 14 years old. When I started, I was just using Taekwondo as something to do for exercise. However, I quickly fell in love with the sport. It helped my physical and mental health, gave me amazing friends, and taught me lifelong skills.

I quickly progressed through the first few belts, receiving a new one every 2 months. Once the pandemic hit, I moved to online classes and followed along through Zoom. While this was difficult, I was still able to learn and progress as I had been before.

By the time I went back to in person taekwondo, I was an advanced student looking to start training for my black belt. I made it through the belts leading up to black belt then trained for months before testing for my black belt. I went to more advanced classes and workshops, some of which were specifically targting one skill we would be tested on. In the summer of 2022, I tested for and received my black belt. While I could have continued to progress to higher degrees, I decided to stop there and focus on school and work.
